JAPANESE GARDENS

Each Japanese garden element has special meanings (e.g., pine trees: strength; circle-shaped pebbles: waves in the ocean and the transience of life; turtle stones: longevity). The top 10 gardens are listed below.
Many people come to Kyoto to escape from the hustle and bustle of big cities but then face the hordes of tourists at the main sites. Luckily, Kyoto is home to several tranquil Japanese gardens, some just a walking distance from the downtown area. The meanings are wary, but rock and Tea ceremony gardens are meant to bring a miniature version of nature to your backyard. Big rocks represent mountains, small rocks represent hills, and pebbles shaped like circles represent ocean waves.
