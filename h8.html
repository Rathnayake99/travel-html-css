Nijo Castle

Nijo Castle (二条城) was built in 1603 at the start of the Edo Period and served as a residence of the Tokugawa Shogunate until it ended in 1867.
The castle is unique as it looks more like a temple than a typical castle. The reason for this unusual design was because it was built during peacetime, so there was no need for significant keeps and an observation tower. If you visit, however, you may find that the floors squeak everywhere, no matter how light you are! This was specially made to detect sneaky ninjas from raiding the castle at night while everyone was sleeping.
The castle closes early at 4 p.m., so visit by 3 p.m. at the latest if you plan on exploring! The Japanese garden requires a separate fee.
